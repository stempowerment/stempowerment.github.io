About the conference

We aim to expose young innovators to new technology

We are a team of high-school students working to curate a conference by youth, for youth on July 1st at 9 AM ET.

We will feature multiple successful teenage speakers, who will be talking about their journeys, and who will provide advice to you so you can conduct research and work on your own projects within STEM.

This is a day full of panels, presentations, and a workshop to help you learn and get further in STEM. We currently have 12 speakers, and 8 sessions, with breaks to ensure ocular rest.

You can join for part of or all of it. We know some people have short attention spans (we do too :)), so come for the sessions you'd like to be there for. Sessions and agenda are below.

Speakers


Mohamad Rajabifard

Mohamad is a 19-year-old engineer and founder. He's co-founded There.so (Pioneer winner) and Subdl.com (with +2 million monthly users). He's co-organized a +600 attendee conference in Europe. At 16 he replaces school with a higher efficiency model for himself.


Isabella Grandic

Izzy is an alternative protein & healthtech innovator. She's currently working on scaling maternal health solutions in rural Nigeria. In the past, she's consulted for alternative protein and tech companies. Her mission is to build a world free of suffering. She's currently the program success manager at The Knowledge Society, a startup building a human accelerator for teenagers.


Adara Hagman

Adara is a 17 y/o researcher, writer, activist, and futurist. She has a huge passion for the environment and for exploration (marine, space, and terrestrial). Adara is on a mission to help address the UN's Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) starting with SDG #13 Climate Action with the project Photome Labs and SDG #2 Zero Hunger with Carbovore. In the past, Adara has spoken at Maple Leaf Foods, Wattpad, and more.


Mhairi McCann

Mhairi is the 21 year old Founder and CEO of Youth STEM 2030, an organization that seeks to empower youth to be at the forefront of progress towards the UN Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) through science, technology, engineering and maths. She has won several science competitions in the past, and has a passion for youth empowerment.


Seyone Chithrananda

Seyone is a curious 16-year-old aspiring ML and biology researcher. He works on researching cutting-edge applications for drug discovery using machine learning, as an ML research intern at the Matter Lab at the University of Toronto. He is on the open-source developer team for DeepChem. He has been working on building COVID small-molecule therapeutics with a lab at UofT alongside MIT.


Liam Hinzman

Liam is a 17 year old machine learning developer on a mission to change the world. He's worked with world-class AI research labs like Layer 6, and some of the leading healthcare institutions including SickKids research hospital.


Alishba Imran

Alishba is a 16-year-old Machine Learning and Blockchain Developer who was recently named Young Innovator to Watch at CES. She's founded Honestblocks, a Blockchain platform to track medication in supply chain in developing countries, working alongside companies such as IBM. Currently, she's working on a company using machine learning to help energy storage companies test their devices in days and speed up development.


Faith Inello

Faith is a 16-year old teenager researching metabolomics and neuroscience, and how they work together. She has a focus on Adverse Childhood Experiences (ACEs) and how they alter the chemical balances in the brain. Additionally, she has founded a company, Neurojoy, to treat mental illness though Brain-Computer Interfaces.


Hailey Vallabh

Hailey Vallabh is a 16-year-old tech enthusiast. About a year ago, she realized that she had to change the way she thought about the world because she was lacking data points that she needed to reach her full potential. That led to her dedicating time to learn about different technologies including synthetic biology and brain-computer interfaces. She is an advocate for women and girls in tech.


Shagun Maheshwari

Shagun is a teenager researching Artificial Intelligence. She has previously worked at XPRIZE and RBC, and she has an ambition to change the way the world works through emerging tech.


Nina Khera

Nina is a longevity and genomics researcher aiming to eradicate aging-related diseases and biological aging. She specializes in senescent cells and has founded her own company, Biotein, to find phenotypes in the mitochondria of these cells. She has been invited to speak about her research at several conferences in the US and Canada.


Andri Kadaifciu

Andri is a biomedical sciences researcher, and scientific writer, determined to find treatments for a wide range of diseases that particularly target the control centers of the body. She is currently working on a research proposal exploring a possible cure for rare skin disease. She is passionate about advocating for girls’ voices and work in STEM.

Competition


Description

We would love to see a video of you explaining a project you’re doing or something you’re passionate about, maximum 10 minutes in length. Be as creative as you want to be -> no other rules or restrictions. Although, we do require clean and PG-rated content.

We will pick 3 videos to be featured on our website, instagram, and to be shown during breaks at our conference!


How to submit

Send it to our email, at stempowermentconference@gmail.com. The subject should be: "Submission" -> Also, make sure you include your name, your topic, and your age.

Submissions will be open from May 25th at 12:01 AM EST to June 25th at 11:59 PM EST.


Timeline

Decisions will be emailed June 28th, and videos/winners will be publicized from then on at our conference and on our social media :).

Support


The Knowledge Society

The Knowledge Society (TKS) is a 10-month accelerator program designed to train kids to innovate in emerging technology and solve the world's biggest problems.


Youth STEM 2030

Youth STEM 2030 provides young people with a platform to share their research, innovations and ideas focusing on the Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) with the world.


Prospective Medical Professionals

Prospective Medical Professionals (PuMP) is a registered federal youth non-profit organization dedicated to helping aspiring high school students connect to health sciences opportunities.


Science and Medicine in Action

They are a non-profit youth-led organization that provides educational, experimental and volunteering opportunities to young Science & Medicine enthusiasts.
